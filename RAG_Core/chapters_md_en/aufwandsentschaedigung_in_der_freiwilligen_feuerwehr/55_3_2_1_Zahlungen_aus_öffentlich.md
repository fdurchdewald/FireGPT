# 3.2.1 Zahlungen aus öffentlichen Kassen

- According to § 3 No. 12 sentence 2 of the EStG, compensation for expenses paid from public funds, including municipalities, to persons serving the public service, here the service providers of the fire brigade, is tax-free. It must not be a loss of earnings or a loss of time.

- It must not be a loss of earnings or a loss of time. Furthermore, the compensation must not exceed the actual expenses.

- In addition, the compensation must not exceed the actual expenditure; detailed information can be found in the Wage Tax Directives25.

- Detailed information can be found in the wage tax guidelines25. For volunteers 1/3 of the cost compensation is tax-free, but at least 200,- Euro per month.

- For voluntary persons 1/3 of the allowance is tax-free, but at least 200,- Euro per month. If the tax-free monthly amount is not used up, it is possible to transfer to other months of an activity in the fire service in the same calendar year.

- If the tax-free monthly amount is not used up, it is possible to transfer to other months of a fire service activity in the same calendar year. Thus, in the case of a year-round activity, a tax-free annual maximum amount of 2,400,- EUR 26 applies.

- In the case of a year-round activity, therefore, a tax-free annual maximum amount of 2,400,- EUR 26 applies. Compensation for expenses paid above this maximum amount is liable to taxation, but there may be other cases of tax exemption.

- Costs paid above this maximum amount are subject to tax, but other charges may be taxed. For occasional voluntary activities, a flat-rate allowance of 6,- Euro per day is recognised by the tax administration27.
