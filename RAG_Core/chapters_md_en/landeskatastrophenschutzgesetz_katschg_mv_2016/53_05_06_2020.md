- 2. A disaster within the meaning of this law is an event which will effectively endanger or damage the lives, health or vital care of many people, animals, the environment or material of such an exceptional value that assistance and protection can only be effectively guaranteed if the competent authorities, bodies, organisations and the forces deployed, under the uniform management of the Civil Protection Authority, interact with each other, including large-scale damage events in individual communities and cities, which entail a significant coordination effort and to which the forces and resources of the local security authorities' providers are not sufficient to combat effectively, but are necessary via local or central means of management and deployment of disaster protection.

- This includes large-scale damage events in individual municipalities and cities, which entail a considerable coordination effort and whose effective fight against the forces and means of the carriers of the local security authorities are not sufficient, but via local or central means of management and deployment of civil protection are necessary. § 2 Carrier of the task (1) Civil protection is the responsibility of the country, the counties and the non-circular cities.
