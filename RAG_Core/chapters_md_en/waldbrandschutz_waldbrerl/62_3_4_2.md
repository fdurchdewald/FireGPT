# 3.4.2

- (a) a forest fire has reached the size of 100 hectares (catastrophic forest fire) or (b) a large forest fire threatens to develop into a disaster forest fire; this is the case if an approximate order of 50 hectares has already been exceeded and the rate of expansion and the endangered stockpiles of tree species and age likely to cause a disaster forest fire; or (c) in the case of a large forest fire there is a tendency to spread and inhabited area or major traffic routes or objects are at risk in such a way that their securing requires a mass-sized set of forces and means corresponding to a disaster case.
