# 5.2.2 Topografie und Boden

- Even if the topographical conditions in Schleswig-Holstein are characterized by lowlands, terrain forms can also affect the vegetation fire propagation here: • Upside slope (heat radiation, thermals, fire property, wind) the fire spreads faster. Downside slope it is rather inhibited.

- Downhill it is rather inhibited. • Areas heated and dried by the sun show a higher fire intensity than areas in the shade, which are rather cool and humid.

- • Areas heated and dried by the sun show a higher fire intensity than areas in the shade, which are rather cool and humid. • Valleys or incisions can channel wind and thus accelerate the fire propagation direction and spread.

- • Valleys or incisions can channel wind and thus accelerate the direction of fire propagation and spread. • Fires in dry or former bog areas (heaths) can run very fast at the most common surface wind driven.

- • Fires in dry or former moored areas (heaths) can run very quickly at the most eve surface. The fire can develop into the flammable soil layers.

- The fire can develop into the combustible layers of the ground. As a result, trees can be overturned without discernable warning or the breaking/sacking of vehicles and persons into burnt layers of the ground.
