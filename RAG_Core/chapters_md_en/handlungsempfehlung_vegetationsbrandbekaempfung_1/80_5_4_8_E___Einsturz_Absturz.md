# 5.4.8 E – Einsturz/Absturz

- The danger of collapse or crash exists in trees that have lost their stability due to the fire action (so-called "Widowmaker", in English: "Witwenmacher").

- "Widowmaker", in English: "Witwenmacher". Through fire action, stones and trees, sometimes also burning, can roll down the slope in steep slopes.

- Through fire action, stones and trees, sometimes also burning, can underroll the slope in steep slopes. This poses a double risk for forces, as this also does not control the spread of the fire.

- This poses a double risk for forces, as it does not control the spread of the fire either. Unknown topography and thus unrecognized crashing can be a danger for forces.

- Unidentified topography and thus unrecognised crashing can be a danger for emergency personnel. In case of an extinguishing water drop by helicopter, trees can be thrown over, and stones and other objects that were taken up during the discharge from the open water may fall down.
