# 2.1 Grundbegriffe Feuerwehrfahrzeuge

- A fire brigade vehicle consists fundamentally of the chassis with a cab, the chassis structure with its equipment rooms and roof boxes as well as the fire-relief technical loading. If fire-extinguishing centrifugal pumps, power generators, pressure mixing (DZA) or compressed air display (DLS) were built in in the vehicle, these represent a fixed fire-relief technical equipment.

- If fire-fighting centrifugal pumps, generators, pressure mixing (DZA) or compressed air showman (DLS) are installed in the vehicle or mechanical towing devices are available, these represent a fixed fire-wehr equipment. Depending on the type of construction, the crew room is part of the chassis or vehicle.

- Depending on the type of construction, the crew room is part of the chassis or the vehicle. In the vehicle structure there are the equipment rooms, whose counting takes place from the cab or crew room beginning towards the rear.

- In the vehicle structure are the equipment rooms, the counting of which takes place from the cab or crew room beginning in the direction of the rear. On the driver side the equipment rooms with an un-directed number are to be found (G1, G3, G5 etc.), on the passenger side the equipment rooms with a straight number (G2, G4, G6 etc.).

- On the driver's side, the unit rooms with a straight number (G1, G3, G5 etc.), on the passenger side, the unit rooms with a straight number (G2, G4, G6 etc.) are to be found. The unit room at the rear of a vehicle is usually referred to as GR (device room back).
