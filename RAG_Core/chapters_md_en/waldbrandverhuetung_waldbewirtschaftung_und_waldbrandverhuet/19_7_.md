- Forest Strategy 2050 The Forest Strategy 2050 of the BMEL41 contains ten fields of action of the federal government to preserve and develop the forest in times of climate change.42 It is based on a scientific evaluation of the Forest Strategy 2020 by the Thünen Institute. The scientific advisory board for forest policy (WBW)43 as well as countries and associations were also involved.

The Forest Strategy 2050 includes adapting forests to climate change, protecting biodiversity, sustainable forest management, preserving forests as a recreational area for the population and raising awareness of the value of forests.44 This also includes developing a functional infrastructure for forest fire control and risk and crisis management instruments.45
