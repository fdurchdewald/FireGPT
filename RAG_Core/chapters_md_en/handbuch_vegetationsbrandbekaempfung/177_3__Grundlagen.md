# 3. Grundlagen

- The VegBbk 24 25 crown and top fires • Fire of the tree crowns • arises from a ground fire and hurries ahead of it (mostly due to the wind or topography) • a permanent crown fire is nourished by a ground fire and cannot exist in the long term without it • characteristic dark grey to black smoke, as there is densely needleed or green fuel in the crowns • by simultaneous flying fire new fires can arise parent fires • describes the burning of individual trunks (e.g. caused by lightning or humans) • Fire usually runs in dry and hollow trunks • usually limited to one or individual trunks • there is a risk that the root fire spreads to the ground fire Full fire • rare and most intense type of forest fire • Fire of the ground and the tree crown • a full fire always arises from a ground fire • dark grey to black smoke, since in the crowns there is densely needleed or green fuel • Flame length depends on the height of the tree herd and, if necessary, on the basis of a flight • can be several metres at the same time.
