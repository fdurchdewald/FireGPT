# 8.2

- Determining and announcing the disaster A catastrophe within the meaning of § 25 ThürBKG is an event in which • the life or health of a large number of people, • the natural basis of life, • significant material values or • the vital supply of the population to an unusual extent is endangered or damaged and the danger can only be prevented if the authorities, departments, organizations, units, institutions and employed forces work together under uniform management.The unified management represents in the case of the overall operational manager (mayor/country councilor or commissioner) with his own associated KatS staff.

- In the case of the General Operations Manager (Mayor/Landrat or Commissioner) the unified management is represented by a corresponding KatS staff. The lower civil protection authority determines the entry (and the end) of the disaster (§ 34 ThürBKG).

- The lower civil protection authority determines the occurrence (and the end) of the disaster (§ 34 ThürBKG). On this basis, the lower KatS authority is guided by the following considerations: • Communication of the disaster case to the higher KatS authorities (TLVwA and TMIK – see Chapter 8.5) • Communication of the disaster to neighbouring KatS authorities (as far as necessary) • Identification of the disaster must be communicated to the public in an appropriate way (e.g. warning apps, press release, regional and supraregional media, loudspeaker vehicles, sirens)
