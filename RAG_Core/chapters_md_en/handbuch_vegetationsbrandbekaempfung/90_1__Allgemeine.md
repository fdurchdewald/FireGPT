# 1. Allgemeine

- Information WBI GLFI 12 13 • Drizzy grasses or a corresponding covering of the soil, including dead wood, increases the risk of forest fire and favours the formation of fire and the spread of a ground fire • especially coniferous trees and such young trees are much more vulnerable to fire than deciduous trees Climate conditions, such as • persistent lack of rainfall, • persistently high air temperatures, • long hours of sunshine during the day, • decreasing soil humidity (below 15%) and • humidity below 50% increase the risk of fires.

- Season/day • in the spring months around April and August, the risk of fire is highest due to the dry vegetation or the climatic situation • Trends show that the danger increases in the morning hours and reaches its peak in the midday to afternoon hours before it decreases again in the early evening hours
