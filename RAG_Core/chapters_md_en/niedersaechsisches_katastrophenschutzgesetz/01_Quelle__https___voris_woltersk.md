- §§ 1 - 4a, First Section - Task and Responsibilities § 1 NKatSG - Civil Protection (1) Civil Protection within the meaning of this law is the preparation of the fight against and the fight against disasters and exceptional events.

- §§ 1 - 4a, Section 1 - Task and responsibilities § 1 NKatSG - Civil protection (1) Civil protection within the meaning of this law is the preparation of the fight against and the fight against disasters and exceptional events. (2) A disaster within the meaning of this law is an emergency situation in which life, health, the vital supply of the population, the environment or significant material values are endangered or impaired to such an extent that its fight by the competent authorities and the necessary emergency and auxiliary personnel requires a central management.

- (2) A disaster within the meaning of this Act is an emergency situation in which life, health, the vital supply of the population, the environment or significant physical assets are at risk or impaired to such an extent that its fight against them by the competent authorities and the necessary operational and auxiliary personnel requires a central management. (3) An exceptional event within the meaning of this Act is a danger to life, health, the vital supply of the population, the environment or significant physical assets which cannot be dealt with by means of local security can result in a disaster and the fight against it requires central support from the competent authorities and the necessary emergency forces and means of disaster protection.

- (3) An exceptional event within the meaning of this law is a risk to life, health, the vital supply of the population, the environment or significant physical assets which can no longer be managed by the means of local security can result in a disaster and the fight against it requires central support from the competent authorities and the necessary forces and means of civil protection.
