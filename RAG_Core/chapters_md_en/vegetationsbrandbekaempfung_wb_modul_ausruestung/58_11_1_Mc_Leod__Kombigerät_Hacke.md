# 11.1 Mc Leod (Kombigerät Hacke-Harke)

- The Mc Leod is for the laying of fire-snacks on a stubble field, as well as in the grass and forest floor. An optimal working length offers the 2 m long handle.

- An optimal working length offers the 2 m long handle. It is therefore very back-friendly for the forces.

- On one side there is a sharp edge (hook) for scratching, hacking, and chipping. On the other side there is a sharp edge (hick) for scratching, hacking, and cutting purpose: • Calculating and scraping the upper surface of the fuel to establish securing lines, or wound strips, e.g. for grass and stubble field fires • Breaking of chaff and vegetation on burnt surfaces • Scratching of the bark of tree trunks • Calculating of the hot coal from tree trunks and stumps.

- On the one hand there is a sharp edge (hook) for scratching, hacking, and cutting purpose: • Deflection and scraping of the above-area fuel for the construction of securing lines, or wound strips, e.g. for grass and stubble field fires • Breaking of chaff and vegetation on burnt-off surfaces • Scratching of the bark of tree trunks • Calculating of the hot coal from tree trunks and stumps. Subsequent use is in the stony and rooted terrain.
