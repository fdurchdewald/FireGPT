# 4.3.2 Organisationseinheiten mit Löschflugzeugen

- Due to its large forest area and the venerable operations of the past few years, the district of Harz has decided to tender a contract with a fire-fighting aircraft operator. The German fire-fighting aircraft rescue squadron (DLFR) operates a constantly ready-to-use fire-fighting aircraft with 2,200 liters of extinguishing water capacity in the district of Harz.

- In the district of Harz, the German fire-fighting aircraft rescue squadron (DLFR) operates a continuously ready-to-use fire-fighting aircraft with 2,200 liters of extinguishing water capacity. Failure reserves and other identical fire-fighting aircraft can be provided via a partner company in Poland (Goldhahn, 2023).

- Failure reserves as well as other identical extinguishing aircraft can be provided via a partner company in Poland (Goldhahn, 2023). Another extinguishing aircraft station is under the leadership of the Lower Saxony State Office for Fire and Civil Protection at Braunschweig Airport.

- Another firefighting aircraft station is under the leadership of the Lower Saxony State Office for Fire and Civil Protection at Braunschweig Airport. Two firefighting aircraft with 3,100 litres of extinguishing agent each are stationed here.

- Two firefighting aircraft with 3,100 litres of extinguishing agent are stationed here. Since these aircraft are supported with considerable EU funds, it is always possible to release them after consultation with the Emergency Response Coor- dination Centre (ERCC) (NLBK, 2023).

- Since these aircraft are supported with considerable EU funds, it is always possible to release them after consultation with the Emergency Response Coor- dination Centre (ERCC) (NLBK, 2023).The fire planes stationed in Germany are ready for use in the forest fire season from April to October and can only be filled with fire water at a landing site.

- The extinguishing aircraft stationed in Germany are ready for use in the forest fire season from April to October and can only be filled with extinguishing water at a landing site. This requires units that carry out the filling process on running machines (Voß, 2023).
