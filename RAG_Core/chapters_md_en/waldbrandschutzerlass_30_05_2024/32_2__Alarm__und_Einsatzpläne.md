# 2. Alarm- und Einsatzpläne

- The local fire protection authorities are obliged, according to § 6 para. 1 number 6 SächsBRKG, to draw up, continue and agree as necessary. For the lower fire protection, rescue service and civil protection authorities the responsibility for the establishment and continuation of foreign alarm and exit regulations as well as extraterritorial operational plans, which also serve the preparation of the fight against major damage events as well as the defense and confirmation of the damages of major damage events by the local fire protection authorities, § 7 para. 1 number 6 SächsBRKG.

- For the lower fire protection, rescue service and civil protection authorities, the responsibility for the establishment and continuation of over-local alarm and exit regulations as well as over-local deployment plans, which also serve the preparation of the fight against major damage events as well as for the defense and prevention of the damages of major damage events by the local fire protection authorities, § 7 para. 1 number 6 SächsBRKG. All civil protection authorities in Saxony also have to prepare and continue the obligation of general civil protection plans as well as special alarm and deployment plans on the basis of hazard and risk analyses according to § 7 para. 1 number 14 SächsBRKG.

- In addition, all civil protection authorities in Saxony have to establish and continue the obligation of general civil protection plans as well as special alarm and operational plans based on risk and risk analyses according to § 7 (1) point 14 SächsBRKG. - In order to meet the requirements of an alert plan, it must contain in particular the following information: • • • • • • • • • • Further proof of the ongoing updating of the alert plan Alarm planning and reporting structure, accommodation and responsibilities of the operational management/management staff Accessibility directory with addresses Warning and information of the population, press and public relations Overviews of operational personnel and operational equipment (type, location, personnel and main equipment) in its own area of responsibility Addresses of other authorities - services, public institutions, information centres 9 • Overviews and contact details of special operational equipment of the BOS • Overviews and contact details of companies for special service tasks and with special vehicles • Destruction planning • Checklists Operations plan While the alarm plan lays down the foundations for a functioning flow of information, the operational plan is to be used for the implementation of concrete determinations of measures to be carried out.

- The most important elements of the deployment plan include: • • • • • • • Taking of threatened special objects (e.g. old-age homes, industrial facilities, hospitals) Preparing security measures (e.g. for gas pipelines, oil tanks, oil pipelines, infrastructure) Measures for areas with special hazards (munition control system; abandoned, non-extinguishing forest areas; open pit areas/slide areas) Preparation and implementation of a possible evacuation (safe return routes) Provision of protected collection points and emergency shelters Creation of fire traps/stop lines Ensure information flow (communication schemes) Networking of alarm and deployment plans Since forest fire events may not limit themselves to a single area of responsibility, it is necessary to coordinate alarm and deployment plans with all the participating organisations, authorities and institutions and neighbouring communities and the over-ordered risk prevention planning at the district level. Update The update of the alarm and deployment plans has to take place regularly.

- Updating The alarm and deployment plans must be updated on a regular basis. It is sensible to plan and carry out an automatic check (e.g. once a year before the forest fire season).

- It is sensible to plan and carry out an automatic control (e.g. once a year before the forest fire season) for this purpose. Basically, an update is to be carried out according to personnel changes, fundamental changes in the administrative structure, major changes in the forest condition or on the basis of new fire fighting and forestry knowledge.
