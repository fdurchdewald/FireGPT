# 1. Wundstreifen

- Wound strips are 2.50 to 3.00 m wide vegetation-free strips, on which the boethe is removed to the mineral floor. They serve to stop ground-fire.

- They are used to stop the firing of soil. The functioning of the wound strips must be ensured by soiling and removal of the fuel.

- The lower forestry authority may, in accordance with § 28 para. 1 of the Forest Act, order the new installation and maintenance of wound strips for the Free State of Saxony (SächsWaldG) in particular in areas of forest fire hazard class A against the forest owners, insofar as these are required in each individual case to ward off the concrete forest fire hazards from railway installations, roads and other areas.

- In accordance with § 28 para. 1 of the Forest Act, the lower forestry authority can order the new plant and the maintenance of wound strips for the Free State of Saxony (SächsWaldG) in particular in areas of forest fire hazard class A against the forest owners, insofar as these are required in each individual case to ward off the concrete forest fire hazards arising from railway installations, roads and other areas. Borders of deciduous trees or mixed stocks with more than 50 per cent of deciduous trees of more than 50 metres depth on railway lines and roads, wound strips are usually unnecessary.

- Limits of deciduous trees or mixed populations with more than 50 percent deciduous tree content of more than 50 meters depth on railway lines and roads, wound strips are usually not necessary. If several forest holdings are concerned, it is at the discretion of the Lower Forestry Authority responsible under § 37 para. 2 sentence 1 SächsWaldG whether and, if so, how it carries out the measure itself after consulting the forest workers.
