# 4.3   Löschwasserversorgung

- 1In fire-prone forest areas, sufficient extinguishing water supply is crucial for successful firefighting. 2A key element of the extinguishing water supply is natural sampling points – especially if they are also suitable for the extinguishing water absorption by helicopters with extinguishing water outer load containers.

- 2An essential element of the extinguishing water supply are natural sampling points – especially if they are also suitable for the extinguishing water absorption by helicopters with extinguishing water external load containers. 3Of the carriers of the defensive fire protection (municipalitys or property owners in community-free areas) has to work for appropriate care and if necessary new installation.

- 3The prevention of fire (municipalities or property owners in community-free areas) must be used to provide adequate care and, if necessary, new facilities. 4When building long-distance water lines in particularly fire-prone forest areas, the municipalities, in consultation with the competent water management authorities, are working to ensure that water collection points (hydronauts) are set up at the operators of the water supply system.

- 4In the construction of pipelines in particularly fire-prone forest areas, the municipalities, in consultation with the competent water management authorities, are working with the operators of the water supply system to ensure that water collection points (hydrogens) are set up. 5Underfloor water collection points must be permanently marked on the site and – as well as overfloor water collection points – entered on the forest fire application maps (see point 4.2).
