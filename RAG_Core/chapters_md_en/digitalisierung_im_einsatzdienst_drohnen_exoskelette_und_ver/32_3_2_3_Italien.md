# 3.2.3 Italien

- In Italy, the fire brigade is governed by a national law for fire and rescue services as well as civil protection and is under the authority of the Interior Ministry in Rome. Regi- onal it is divided into fire brigade commandos of 100 individual provinces and their associated control stations.

- It is divided into fire brigade commandos of 100 individual provinces and their associated control stations. The approximately 35,000 Italian fire brigade forces consist in large parts of full-time staff, which are supported by a small number of volunteers and spread over 500 fire guards throughout Italy.

- The approximately 35,000 Italian firefighters are mostly full-time employees, supported by a small number of volunteers and spread over 500 fire stations throughout Italy. The autonomous provinces of South Tyrol, Aostetal and Trento, which have regulated fire services in their own state law, are an exception.

- The autonomous provinces of South Tyrol, Aostetal and Trento, which have regulated the fire service sector in their own state law, are an exception. Their fire service structure with almost exclusively voluntary forces and principal officials only in conurbations, is strongly based on that of the German fire brigades [20] [21].
