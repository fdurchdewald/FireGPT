# 3.2.4 Frankreich

- Since 1996, the French fire brigades have been divided into so-called departments (regions) with state legislation. Each of these departments has its own fire brigade structure with its own control centre.

- Each of these departments has its own fire department structure with its own control centre. Here, the municipalities are free to entertain their own fire department or to join forces with other communities.

- The municipalities are free to maintain their own fire brigades or to join forces with other communities, with the exception of the fire brigades in Paris and Marseille, which are subordinated to the military.

- The fire brigades in Paris and Marseille are an exception, as they are subordinated to the military. Essentially, the fire brigades have been given the task of carrying out the statutory tasks for security and maintenance.

- In essence, the fea- tures have been entrusted with the statutory tasks for security and the maintenance service. Furthermore, this organisational structure allows centralized training and material procurement for the forces of the voluntary and professional fire brigades [22].
