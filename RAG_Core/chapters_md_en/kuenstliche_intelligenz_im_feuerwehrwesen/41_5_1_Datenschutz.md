# 5.1 Datenschutz

- With the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the European Union introduced in 2018 a guarantee for the protection of personal data within the European Union.9 It covers the processing of personal data which is wholly, partially or not automated. However, the GDPR provides different conditions for legitimate processing, hereinafter the most important for the fire brigade: • If the data subject consents.10 • Necessity to fulfil a legal obligation.11 • Necessity to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another natural person.12 • Necessity of processing for the performance of a task in the public interest or in the exercise of public authority conferred on the controller.13 The above conditions show that there are exceptions to legal processing, however, whether these should be examined in detail [52].

- However, the GDPR provides for different conditions for lawful processing, hereinafter the most important for the fire department: • If the data subject consents.10 • Necessity to fulfil a legal obligation.11 • Necessity to protect the vital interests of the data subject or another natural person.12 • Necessity of processing for the performance of a task in the public interest or in the exercise of public authority conferred on the controller.13 The above conditions show that there are exceptions for lawful processing, however, whether these should be examined in detail [52]. To the extent that the assignment of data to certain persons is not necessary, the alternative of pseudonymisation of data sets is to ensure the protection of personal data.

- Insofar as it is not necessary to assign the data to specific persons, the alternative of pseudonymizing data sets is to ensure the protection of personal data. Here, the assignment of data to a person, by removing names and identification features, is made more difficult or prevented [53].
