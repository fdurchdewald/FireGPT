# 2.7.4 Ausbildung der Piloten und Luftfahrzeugbesatzungen

- The pilots and aircraft crews should be trained and instructed in the tactics and techniques for the respective use. Basics on the fire behaviour of vegetation fires as well as the simple tactical forest fire forecast should also be trained.

- Basics on the fire behaviour of vegetation fires as well as the simple tactical forest fire forecast should also be trained. If a corresponding tactical training of the aircraft crews is not available in fire fighting, appropriately trained personnel (e.g. air observers, flight assistants) must be used by the fire brigades for expert advice – if necessary on board the aircraft.

- If the appropriate tactical training of the aircraft crews is not available in firefighting, appropriately trained personnel (e.g. air observers, flight assistants) must be used by the fire brigades for expert advice – if necessary on board the aircraft. Cooperation with the fire brigade should be practised regularly, taking into account the respective responsibilities – and subject to availability of aircraft capacities.

- Cooperation with the fire brigade should be practised regularly, taking into account the respective responsibilities and the availability of aircraft capacities. Similar to other countries (e.g. Australia10), it is recommended to establish common minimum agreements to enable safe and efficient use.

- Similar to the other countries (e.g. Australia10), it is recommended to establish common minimum agreements to enable safe and efficient use. Currently, there is no uniform European-wide or German-wide legal regulation on the requirements for aircraft crews in the field of vegetation fire control.
